To those that don't know me, I am a computational linguistics student. Some of you may know it as Natural Language Processing (NLP). To clear a misconception, those that traditionally study linguistics analyze aspects such as morphology, psycholinguistics, phonetics, and syntax. Studying these aspects of language does not necessarily mean one learns to speak or write these languages.

Now that is established, I have also studied Vietnamese, Mandarin Chinese, and Japanese due to my own interest. Learning languages has been useful in understanding the cultural differences that exist between members of different societies. However, language offers so much more.

Languages are powerful tools that encapsulate information about the world around us. It's an invaluable resource that has allowed people of the modern era to build upon the foundation that earlier societies established for us. It enables the communication between people. It allows people to create and distribute information. It is an instrument that gives people access to power and money. It is a tool that allows us to make decisions, small and large. Language is the medium through which we make sense of the world around us. It enables us to create the meaning we are given in society. Language is also a way to communicate with ourselves and allows us to connect to our emotions. In my mind, language is without a doubt one of the most important aspects of human society.

Though I study linguistics, I also have an interest in computer science. The field of computer science is extremely robust; its development has promoted accessibility to information and resources on a worldwide scale. As these technologies evolve, so does the demand for seamless integration between people and machines. GPT-3 is a language model that boasts robust parameters, which attempts to emulate written and spoken language patterns. To facilitate this integration, OpenAI and Microsoft use Artificial Intelligence (AI) and deep learning artificial neural networks to process 175 billion parameters and 800 GB of storage in their training. Though it is 800 GB, keep in mind that this storage is used to parse text.

Despite this, current language models still suffer from a lack of consistency between the input and output language. Often, there is a disconnect between the literal and implied meaning of a given utterance or sentence. People may have the capacity to identify these disconnects, but often we cannot articulate these ideas. If it were that simple, people would be able to walk around with Google translate attached to their faces and be understood by those around them. Even if this magical device were to somehow work flawlessly, people are also subject to being unable to express themselves with their words properly.

This is where those like myself come in, in pursuit of bridging that gap. By finally getting around to developing this website, I'm hoping to record my thoughts and findings on the matter over the years in my journey to understanding the world and the rich information that other people and societies have to offer and develop a seamless integration between the human experience and the solutions that technology has to offer.
